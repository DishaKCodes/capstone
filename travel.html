How Do Reach Vrindavan?

By Airways

The nearest airport to Vrindavan is Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL) in New Delhi, which is about 142 kilometers away.

See Flights

By Roadways

If you are coming from Delhi, you can take the Yamuna Expressway or NH44. The drive is approximately 3-4 hours.

Book a cab

By Railways

The nearest major railway station to Vrindavan is Mathura Junction, which is about 14 kilometers
away.

Reservations
Tip:

If you are planning to visit during a festival or holiday season, it’s advisable to book your travel and accommodation in advance due to high demand.

Destinations To Visit In Vrindavan...

Banke Bihari Temple

This temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna, who is also known as Banke Bihari. It is one of the most revered temples in Vrindavan and attracts thousands of devotees daily. The deity of Lord Krishna here is known for his playful and mischievous appearance.

ISKCON Temple (Krishna Balaram Mandir)

Built by the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), this temple complex is dedicated to Lord Krishna and his elder brother Balarama. It is famous for its beautiful architecture, serene atmosphere, and regular chanting and worship ceremonies.

Prem Mandir

Also known as the "Temple of Love," Prem Mandir is a relatively newer addition to the religious landscape of Vrindavan. It is adorned with intricate marble carvings depicting the pastimes of Radha and Krishna, and it is illuminated beautifully in the evenings.

Radha Raman Temple

This temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna, who is worshiped here in the form of a small deity known as Radha Raman. It is one of the most ancient temples in Vrindavan and is known for its historical and spiritual significance.
